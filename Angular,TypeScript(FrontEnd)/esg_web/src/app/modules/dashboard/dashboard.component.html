Frameworks

Global Reporting Initiative is an independent, international organization that helps businesses and other organizations take responsibility for their impacts, by providing them with a global common language to communicate those impacts.
The Sustainability Accounting Standards Board (SASB) is an independent non-profit, which guides the disclosure of financially material sustainability information by companies to their investors. Available for 77 industries, the Standards identify the subset of environmental, social, and governance issues most relevant to financial performance in each industry.
The Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD), is a guidance framework that helps companies disclose climate-related financial risks to investors, lenders, and insurers. TCFD recommendations are focused on governance, strategy, risk management, metrics, and targets.
The Carbon Disclosure Project is a not-for-profit charity that runs a global disclosure system for investors, companies, cities, states, and regions to manage their environmental impacts, by focusing on Energy Consumption, GHG Emissions, Power Generation, and Water data.
Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting has been made mandatory for the top 1,000 listed companies by market capitalization from FY 2022-23 onwards. BRSR lays considerable emphasis on quantifiable metrics, which will enable easier comparability across sectors and time periods.
The SGX ESG Framework encourages transparency and accountability, fostering responsible business behaviors that align with global sustainability goals. It facilitates businesses in navigating the intricacies of regional and global sustainability challenges while aligning with the specific needs of the Singaporean market.

The United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (UNSDGs) are a set of 17 goals aimed at addressing global challenges and promoting sustainable development worldwide, including poverty eradication, education, gender equality, climate action, and sustainable cities, highlighting the interconnectedness of social, economic, and environmental issues.
